30-SECOND OPENER (memorize verbatim)

“MindMatch solves the #1 problem in starting therapy: fit anxiety. We match patients to psychologists on the dimensions APA’s Task Force on Evidence-Based Therapy Relationships classifies as Demonstrably Effective — your preferences, how you want to be helped, culture, and goal alignment — and we de-risk the choice with swap protection: if it’s not the right fit in the first 3 sessions, the patient transfers their unused package to another therapist at no extra cost. We’re starting in Lisbon.”

Why this works: preempts the #1 reviewer note (your differentiator got lost last time). Leads with the problem, not the tech.

IF ASKED “What’s your matching framework?” (Anne-Laure WILL ask)

“We match on the dimensions that have the strongest empirical evidence in psychotherapy research — specifically the four matching variables the APA Task Force on Evidence-Based Therapy Relationships (Divisions 12 + 29, Norcross & Wampold) classifies as ‘Demonstrably Effective’: patient preferences, reactance level, culture, and religion/spirituality.

Operationally, three layers:

  1. Structured filters — therapy approach, language, gender, location, price (covers preferences + culture).
  2. A guided 5-question conversational intake that surfaces what brought the person to therapy, prior experience, communication preference, and how directive vs collaborative they want the therapist (covers reactance + goal consensus).
  3. Verified post-session feedback — patients rate therapists on the qualities the evidence says heal: empathy, warmth, goal collaboration. The Task Force classifies collecting client feedback as Demonstrably Effective. Swap protection is the behavioral safety net: if it’s not the right fit in 3 sessions, the package transfers free.

So we surface therapists who fit your preferences AND who consistently embody the relational qualities the evidence shows actually drive outcomes — empathy, warmth, and goal alignment.”

Killer move: “APA Task Force” + “Demonstrably Effective” is the strongest possible scientific language. Lead with what you DO — don’t waste oxygen on what you don’t do.

EVIDENCE BASE (memorize the bold numbers)

From Laska, Gurman & Wampold (2014), Psychotherapy 51(4), Table 1 — % of outcome variance explained by each factor:

Factor % variance Source
Goal consensus / collaboration 11.5% Tryon & Winograd 2011 meta-analysis
Empathy 9.0% Elliott et al. 2011 meta-analysis
Therapeutic alliance 7.5% Horvath et al. 2011 / Flückiger 2018
Positive regard 7.3% Farber & Doolin 2011
Congruence / genuineness 5.7% Kolden et al. 2011
Therapist differences 5.0% Baldwin & Imel 2013
Specific treatment technique <1.0% Wampold & Imel 2015

Combined common-relationship factors ≈ 30% of variance. Specific treatment technique < 1%. Patient/extratherapeutic factors estimated ~40% (Lambert 1992).

APA Div 12/29 Task Force official conclusion (verbatim, quotable):

“The therapy relationship makes substantial and consistent contributions to psychotherapy outcome independent of the specific type of treatment. The therapy relationship accounts for why clients improve (or fail to improve) at least as much as the particular treatment method.”

Matching dimensions classified “Demonstrably Effective”: Patient Preferences, Reactance/Resistance Level, Culture, Religion/Spirituality.

Matching dimensions classified “Probably Effective”: Stages of Change, Coping Style.
